Features

Features are a deceptively simple concept in machine learning. In this article, I would like to go through the different ways I have thought about features.

Table Perspective

One way to think about features are as column headers. Consider a table of data:

Height (inches) Weight (pounds) Age (years)
65 150 25
70 180 30
68 160 28

The "features" here are the column headers. In particular, they are the words themselves and nothing else (later we will discuss perspectives where a feature is more than just some text on a page).

Features are generally descriptive enough that one can understand the meaning of the data in their column. However, one can use have non-descriptive features, as exemplified below. The meaning of the data will have to be explained separately.

A B C
65 150 25
70 180 30
68 160 28
